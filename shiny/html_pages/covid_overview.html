The Johns Hopkins Epidemiologic and Economic Model: COVID-19 Pandemic Projections

A Mathematical Model of HIV Transmission that Explores the Potential Impacts on HIV Control from the COVID-19 Pandemic

The JHEEM is a mathematical model of HIV transmission calibrated to US cities that generates local predictions of the HIV epidemic. In this application, we generate projections - under different scenarios of how the pandemic might affect sexual transmission, viral suppression, HIV testing, and pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) use - to forecast possible effects of the pandemic on HIV transmission.

Projected HIV Incidence for Baltimore, MD with and without Pandemic

Orange lines show projections if the COVID-19 pandemic had never occurred; blue lines indicate projections under a range of pandemic effects on sexual transmission, viral suppression, HIV testing, and PrEP use.

Getting Started:

Prerun Scenarios let you quickly visualize projections for scenarios of pandemic effects that we have already defined and run ahead of time.

Custom Scenarios let you simulate - in real time - different time frames for the pandemic's effects as well as different effects on viral suppression among PWH, HIV testing, and PrEP use across to demographic subgroups of your choosing.
